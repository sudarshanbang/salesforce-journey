# What is CRM?

CRM (Customer Relationship Management) is a strategy and software solution that helps businesses manage customer information, sales activities, and customer relationships.

### Benefits

- Better customer relationships
- Increased productivity
- Sales tracking
- Customer data management
- Improved customer support

---

# What is Salesforce?

Salesforce is a cloud-based Customer Relationship Management (CRM) platform that enables businesses to manage sales, service, marketing, and automation.

Founded: 1999

Founder: Marc Benioff

---

# Cloud Computing

Cloud computing delivers computing resources over the internet.

Instead of maintaining physical infrastructure, businesses use cloud services on demand.

### Cloud Models

- SaaS
- PaaS
- IaaS

---

## SaaS

Software delivered over the internet.

Examples:

- Salesforce
- Gmail
- Microsoft 365

---

## PaaS

Platform used to develop applications.

Example:

Salesforce Platform

---

## IaaS

Infrastructure provided through the cloud.

Examples:

- AWS EC2
- Azure Virtual Machine

---

# Salesforce Terminology

## App

A collection of objects and functionality designed for a specific business process.

Examples:

- Sales App
- Service App

---

## Object

An object stores business data.

### Types

- Standard Object
- Custom Object

Examples:

- Account
- Contact
- Lead
- Opportunity

---

## Record

A single entry stored inside an object.

---

## Field

A specific piece of information stored within a record.

Examples:

- Name
- Phone
- Email
- Industry

---

# Standard Objects

## Lead

Represents a potential customer.

## Account

Represents a company or organization.

## Contact

Represents an individual person associated with an Account.

## Opportunity

Represents a potential sales deal.

# Key Takeaways

- Salesforce is the world's leading CRM platform.
- Salesforce follows the SaaS model.
- Objects store business data.
- Records are individual entries inside objects.
- Fields store specific information about records.
- Standard objects support common sales processes.
